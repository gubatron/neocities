Practice makes perfect! This is what I do to get better at art and keep up a variety in my works. Each entry below represents a study or a collection of pieces focused on a specific skill. Click any image to view it and cycle through the process photos.

Top Three Best Social Camouflage Techniques for Emotionally Dysregulated At-Fault Individuals (Results May Vary) 2025
What are the most effective ways to externalize and materialize complex emotional phenomena (such as imposter syndrome, default guilt, and alexithymia) through physical art forms like soft sculpture/plushies, in a way that aligns with my personal artistic expression and emotional experience? I had to relearn a lot of sewing skills and make a test dummy first in order to try them out. Lots of mishaps but in the end I'm happy with it.
We Remember Because We Cannot Forget 2025
How can memory, generational trauma, and national identity be expressed through re-imagined wartime portraits, where the absurdity and tragedy of war are heightened by replacing human figures with simplified cartoon cats? By contrasting innocence and brutality, cartoon simplicity and historical realism, my work seeks to explore how the past continues to shape the present, how empathy transcends lived experience, and how remembrance can become both an act of mourning and of pride.
Cardboard Display Scene 2024-2025
The prompt was to draw two people eating at an all-you-can-eat buffet, located underwater, celebrating your birthday. This was made with cardboard, purple glue stick glue, colored pencils of varying sizes, one pencil, and 3 stickers. The “all you can eat buffet” is the accumulated marine snow on the sea floor and the “birthday cake” is a pile of marine snow. You can have as much marine snow as you want! I had to put them in deep sea exploration suits because they'd immediately die from the pressure, but I think you can still celebrate and/or eat in them. No hot glue, so I had to carve holes into the cardboard to slot in pieces and use my purple glue stick. It was a time limited ordeal, so no process photos. Very fun to make!
